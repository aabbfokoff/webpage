Thursday

Thursday is the hardest day for me because I have a lot of lectures and I usually have to go to work. Again, I want to mention one of today's courses - formal grammar.

Like mathematical logic, it is a very difficult subject to grasp. And just like mathematical logic, grammar is the course on which it is based - automaton theory. Automata theory is most closely related to algorithm theory: the automaton converts discrete information in stages into discrete time points and generates the result in steps of a given algorithm. Finding the right algorithm and putting it on paper is the hardest part of these machines. With formal grammar, everything is very similar - you also need to find the right algorithms and write and draw them on paper.

The solution of automation theory and formal grammar exercises is also very helpful in the programming profession. Mostly it collects the brain in a pile and causes it to think in different directions.

To pass the course, you must successfully complete two tests and also complete the weekly homework required. That way, you need to think about slots and grammar every week. This way the subject is not forgotten, and every time you learn it, more and more materials are remembered.
